Sub-Millimetre Web Defect Detection at 800 Metres Per Minute

The Problem Statement

At industrial winding speeds exceeding 15 meters per second, microscopic web defects such as pinholes, scratches, and structural wrinkles are completely invisible to the human eye. If left undetected, these anomalies compromise the physical barrier properties of metallized packaging films, leading to costly raw-material wastage and extensive shipping batch rejections by export clients. Traditional area-scan cameras fail entirely in these high-speed settings due to extreme motion blur, frame drop gaps, and insufficient sensor synchronization under high-frequency production vibration. Factory managers were forced to rely on manual, random post-roll inspections that missed critical runs and delayed shipment authorization.

The Scenviz Approach

To resolve this high-speed QA bottleneck, Scenviz engineered a custom, dual-channel optical line-scan inspection system retrofitted directly onto the active slitting plane. The layout utilizes four 8K high-resolution line-scan cameras: two mounted in a direct light-transmission configuration to capture background light leaks, and two set in a dark-field configuration to capture surface structural deformations and minor wrinkles. The entire optical array is synchronized directly to the physical rollers via shaft encoders triggered at a continuous frequency of 75 kHz. This hardware integration maintains a consistent spatial resolution of 0.183 mm per pixel across the entire web length. The local edge GPU cluster processes the raw pixel streams within 1.2 milliseconds, identifying and locating anomalies without requiring any line speed reductions.

The Scenviz high-speed line-scan deployment completely transformed quality assurance on the active production floor, replacing reactive manual checks with 100% automated surface inspection. Operating continuously at line speeds of 800–900 meters per minute (15 m/s), the system segments and classifies defects into six distinct structural classes—including pinholes, wrinkles, and vertical scratches—without introducing a single millisecond of line lag.

Each detected anomaly is instantly logged in local, air-gapped database tables and mapped to a physical roll encoder timestamp. This allows downstream operators to easily locate and splice out flawed material before final roll packaging, reducing physical material waste by 18% per shift. The resulting digital roll quality certificates are automatically signed and stored, providing transparent quality logs that export buyers accept as regulatory-grade proof of product integrity. By securing 100% inspection coverage, the manufacturer has completely eliminated export batch rejections, saving significant operational outlay and establishing an unassailable reputation for material precision with global packaging clients.
